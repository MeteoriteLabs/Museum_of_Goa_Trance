
PROTECT THE BIRTHPLACE: PRESERVE GOA’S TRANCE HERITAGE AS A LIVING CULTURAL LEGACY






WHY THIS PETITION MATTERS
For more than five decades, Goa has been globally recognized as the birthplace of trance music — a cultural movement that began on its shores and evolved into a worldwide phenomenon influencing music, tourism, art, and creative communities across more than 100 countries.



Very few places in the world can claim to be the birthplace of an entire music genre.

Jazz emerged from New Orleans.
Techno began in Detroit.
Trance music was born in Goa.

What began as small music gatherings on Goa’s beaches in the 1960s and 70s grew into a global cultural ecosystem that now spans continents and generates significant tourism and creative economic value worldwide.

Today, however, this living cultural heritage faces the risk of gradual disappearance from its own birthplace.

This petition calls for the responsible recognition, preservation, and structured support of Goa’s trance heritage as a globally significant cultural legacy.

 
QUICK FACTS: THE ECONOMIC AND CULTURAL REALITY
Tourism Contribution: Tourism contributes approximately 16.43% to Goa’s GSDP and supports nearly 40–45% of employment directly and indirectly.

Tourism Employment: Approximately 2.5 lakh people in Goa depend on tourism for their livelihoods.

Record Tourism Volume: Goa recorded more than 1.08 crore tourist arrivals in 2025.

Foreign Tourist Decline: Foreign arrivals dropped from approximately 8.9 lakh in 2017 to around 5.2 lakh in 2025 — indicating a decline in high-value international visitors.

Charter Tourism Decline: Charter flights, historically the backbone of winter international tourism: 2024: 266 charter flights / 58,680 tourists
2025: 189 charter flights / 40,336 tourists

High-value international cultural tourists are historically the longest-staying and highest-spending segment. Protecting the cultural ecosystem that attracts them is directly linked to Goan livelihoods.

 
A CULTURAL TIMELINE: FROM GOA TO THE WORLD
1960s–70s: Early cultural gatherings begin on Goa’s beaches as international travelers, musicians, and artists converge.
1970s–80s: Distinct Goa sound and dance culture begins to form through fusion of global electronic music and local cultural influences.
1990s: Travelers carry the Goa sound worldwide; first major international festivals inspired by Goa emerge in Europe.
2000s: Global trance festival ecosystem expands across Europe, South America, Australia, and Asia.
2010s–Present: International festival economy grows significantly, while Goa faces increasing pressure on its original cultural spaces.

Today, festivals across Portugal, Hungary, Brazil, Croatia, Thailand, and beyond trace their cultural lineage back to Goa.














GLOBAL ECONOMIC FOOTPRINT: A WORLDWIDE INDUSTRY ROOTED IN GOA
The global trance and transformational festival ecosystem that originated from Goa now generates significant economic value worldwide.

















Example: Boom Festival (Portugal) has been cited as generating approximately €50–56 million in local economic impact during a single edition.

Similar festivals across Europe and South America attract tens of thousands of international visitors annually and generate substantial tourism revenue.

Conservative modeled estimates of the global trance ecosystem:

Direct festival economy: approximately $150M–$280M annually (tickets, vendors, merchandise, partnerships)
Tourism spillover: hundreds of millions annually including travel, accommodation, local spending
Lifetime global cultural footprint: multiple billions of dollars over three decades influenced by Goa-origin culture















While Goa pioneered this culture, other countries now capture a growing share of the economic value.

Goa should not be the one place where the source culture fades while the rest of the world continues to thrive on what began here.

 
GOA TOURISM TODAY: RECORD NUMBERS BUT CHANGING PROFILE
Goa’s tourism numbers remain strong in total volume but have shifted in composition.

The state now receives large domestic tourist volumes, but the long-stay international cultural segment — historically associated with Goa’s global music and arts identity — has declined.

International cultural travelers: Stay longer
Spend more per day
Support local businesses, guesthouses, taxis, and artisans
Promote Goa globally

Protecting the heritage that attracts this segment strengthens Goa’s long-term tourism value and brand identity.

 
INDIA’S GLOBAL CULTURAL SOFT POWER
Trance culture in Goa has always represented more than entertainment. It evolved through artistic exchange and was influenced by India’s long traditions of music, rhythm, philosophy, and community.

For many international visitors, Goa became their first deep encounter with Indian spirituality, artistic openness, and cultural exchange.

This represents a unique form of India’s global cultural soft power — where music, travel, and culture intersect.

Preserving this heritage strengthens India’s global cultural presence.

 
A GLOBAL LESSON: FROM DETROIT TO BERLIN
History offers a clear precedent.

Techno music originated in Detroit, USA. However, due to lack of structured support and declining infrastructure, much of its cultural and economic center shifted to Berlin.

Berlin embraced the culture, supported venues and creative spaces, and today is globally recognized as a capital of electronic music tourism and creative economy.

Detroit remains the birthplace — but Berlin became the global center.

Goa now stands at a similar turning point.

If the original cultural ecosystem continues to weaken, the global center of gravity reminded of Goa’s heritage may permanently shift elsewhere. Cultural ecosystems rarely return once displaced.

 
A RESPONSIBLE PATH FORWARD
This petition does not call for unregulated activity or disregard for community and environmental concerns.

It calls for balanced, responsible, and forward-looking cultural preservation.

We respectfully request:

Recognition of Goa as the birthplace of global trance culture
Documentation and preservation through a museum or archive initiative
Balanced policy frameworks supporting responsible cultural tourism
Designated and well-managed cultural or music zones where appropriate
Environmental and sustainability standards
Dialogue between local communities, policymakers, tourism stakeholders, artists, and organizers

With proper documentation and preservation, Goa’s trance heritage could in the future be positioned for international cultural recognition pathways similar to how other cities have preserved electronic music heritage.

 
FINAL APPEAL
Goa gave the world a sound and a spirit that brought people together across cultures and generations.

Protecting this heritage is not only about music — it is about preserving a globally significant cultural legacy that originated in India and continues to support Goan livelihoods and global cultural exchange.

Preserve the culture.
